Scaling a RAG-Powered AI Assistant to 2.3M Monthly Queries
I owned the full ML product lifecycle — feature engineering, deployment, monitoring, and iteration — for an enterprise conversational AI assistant. The result: accuracy from 70% to 92%, answer rate from 45% to 80%, <1% hallucination rate, and $6.8M in annual savings.
The Challenge
  • Question answer rate stuck at 45% — more than half of queries unresolved, driving up support costs and eroding user trust
  • Model accuracy at 70% insufficient for regulatory approval; hallucination risk unacceptable in compliance-sensitive environment
  • No systematic LLM evaluation framework existed; model selection and prompt iteration were ad hoc and undocumented
What I Did
  • Conducted 20+ user research interviews and analyzed 10,000 support tickets to identify query failure modes and improvement priorities
  • Designed structured evaluation of 8 LLMs — selected GPT-4, reducing API costs 34% while improving response quality 25%
  • Built 5-layer production monitoring: accuracy, latency, hallucination rate, drift detection, and CSAT — enabling rapid, evidence-based iteration
  • Defined and hit explicit success criteria: accuracy ≥92%, latency <2s, cost <$50K/month — all three achieved simultaneously
  • Grew data science team 4→8 specialists, achieving 92% engagement and 95% on-time delivery across $2.5M annual budget

ML-Powered Document Classification for 1.2M Legal Documents
Led end-to-end product delivery of a machine learning document labeling system — automatically classifying 1.2M legal documents, aligning 20+ international stakeholders, and delivering 17% efficiency improvement through intelligent automation.
The Challenge
  • Program officers manually reviewed and classified massive volumes of legal documents — slow, inconsistent, and impossible to scale
  • High-stakes grant decisions depended on accurate document categorization; misclassification had real consequences for program integrity
  • 20+ stakeholders across global geographies with competing taxonomies and priorities needed to align before any model could be trained
What I Did
  • Facilitated structured requirements workshops with 20+ global stakeholders to define shared classification taxonomy and labeling logic
  • Wrote product requirements for multi-label NLP classification model with human-in-the-loop escalation for low-confidence predictions
  • Designed confidence threshold framework ensuring high-risk documents always routed to human review — balancing automation with oversight
  • Built stakeholder reporting layer enabling program officers to audit model decisions and flag edge cases for retraining
